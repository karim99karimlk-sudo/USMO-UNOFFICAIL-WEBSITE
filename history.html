USMO History

Introduction

Union Sportive Musulmane Oujda also known by USMO, One of the most historic Moroccan clubs .Founded in 1958 in the heart of Oujda, Morocco. The club's primary colours are black and white , The fans nicknamed the team by "The Stork of the East". Currently USMO is playing in the second division BOTOLA PRO 2.

How was USMO founded?

The story began when the three friends: AbdelRahman Belaouchi, Benyounes Mezouari and Boumadien Betmouchent , came up with an idea of establishing a club after seeing the vibrant energy of youth in their neighborhood "CASABLANCA ROAD". The club's headquarters that time was in the Oulad Mimoun Cafe.

At first, they made Hajj Mimoun Moumen an honorary president after convincing him, Mekki Bouchaib as a talent scout , Hajj Elaarbi Ben Lakhder Darfoufi nicknamed by "Ba Aroub" as a coach , He is considered the first team coach for Union .

USMO Beginnings

The season 62/63 Division 3 USMO Qualified to Division 2 for the first time after winning the last match against khouribga. The season after, the team had been relegated but they didn't give up , They worked hard. They united. They became unstoppable that season they didn't lose not even a single match and reterning back again to Division 2 65/66. Two years later, specifically in the 68/69 season, the team was so close from promotion to the first professional division ,finished second in the overall standings of the Northern Division 2 with 51 points, 3 points behind Salé Association, which had been promoted . In the same season, the USMO's youth team won the national championship, ahead of Union Casablanca.

The Golden Era

In the 1974/75 season, the team was relegated to division 3 for 6 seasons, before returning to the Second Division in the 1980/81 Season. In the opinion of all Old fans that the 1980s are considered the golden age of the USMO team. In the 1981/82 season, they reached the historic play-off for the first professional division against Ittihad Touarga. The team also reached the semi-finals of the Throne Cup in 1987, losing 2-1 to their neighbors, Renaissance Berkane in Rabat Stadium Moulay Abdellah.
